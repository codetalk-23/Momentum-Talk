Apply these rules to every designed output. No exceptions. Do not improvise colors or fonts outside this system.

Rules:

Forest green (#1A3D2B) is the primary accent — use on labels, key stats, logo, highlights
Green Mid (#2D6A4F) is allowed as a secondary accent when variety is needed
No gradients. No shadows. No full black (#000000) backgrounds.
No green buttons or green card fills — green is for labels and accents only
Backgrounds are white (#FFFFFF) or warm grey (#F5F4F0) only

Logo
Two logo files are available in assets/:

Logo_Black_on_White.png — use on white/light backgrounds
Logo_Black_transparent.png — use when you need a transparent background

When to use the logo:

Title slides, cover pages, proposal headers, external documents, email banners
Do NOT put the logo on every page — once per document is sufficient
Never recolor the logo. Never apply effects, shadows, or outlines to it.

Logo on color: Place on Forest green (#1A3D2B) for dark-background variants (the brand guide shows this as the official "op forest" usage).

Hero Image / Banner
assets/baner-momentum-volvo.png — a wide landscape photo (Dutch/Scandinavian polder with windmill, Momentum-branded Volvo in Forest green driving through).
When to use:

Hero sections on proposals, landing pages, or pitch decks (title/cover only)
Can be cropped to different aspect ratios: wide banner (16:3), square (1:1), portrait, etc.
Use as a full-bleed background with white or near-black text overlaid
Do NOT use on every page — it's a statement image, use once per document

Crop guidance:

Full-width banner: use the full image, text sits upper-left or centered above horizon
Portrait/square: crop to center-right where the car and windmill are visible


Layout Principles

White space first — generous padding, clean layouts, nothing cramped
Grid: left-aligned content, consistent margins
Cards: white background or warm grey (#F5F4F0), with border (#E8E8E8) at 1px
Photos: only on title/cover and final/closing slides or sections. Not mid-content.
No decorative elements — no clip art, no stock icons, no gradients, no patterns
No green fills — green is text/label color only, not background fills

Infographics — Extended Color Palette

Key rule: Infographics use a dedicated 5-pair / 10-color palette (Sage, Forest Muted, Champagne, Caramel, Lilac, Dusty Purple, Powder Blue, Slate Blue, Petal, Muted Red). This palette is only for infographics — it does not replace the standard brand palette for other outputs.
Always read these references first and model the output on the example HTML before writing any code.
